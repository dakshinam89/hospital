Appointments

How to Schedule an Appointment

Learn how to schedule appointments through our website, by phone, or in person.

Online Scheduling:

  1. Visit our Appointment page
  2. Fill out the appointment request form
  3. Select your preferred doctor, date, and time
  4. Submit the form and wait for confirmation

Phone Scheduling:

  1. Call our appointment line at (123) 456-7890
  2. Provide your information to the receptionist
  3. Discuss available dates and times
  4. Receive your appointment confirmation

Rescheduling or Canceling Appointments

Need to change your appointment? Here's how to reschedule or cancel.

To reschedule or cancel an appointment:

  • Log in to your patient portal account
  • Navigate to "My Appointments"
  • Select the appointment you wish to change
  • Choose "Reschedule" or "Cancel"

Alternatively, you can call our appointment line at (123) 456-7890.

Note: Please provide at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations to avoid a cancellation fee.

Insurance & Billing

Understanding Your Medical Bill

A guide to reading and understanding your hospital bill.

Your medical bill contains several key sections:

  • Patient Information: Your personal and insurance details
  • Service Summary: List of services provided with dates
  • Charges: Itemized list of charges for each service
  • Insurance Payments: What your insurance covered
  • Patient Responsibility: The amount you owe

If you have questions about your bill, please contact our billing department at (123) 456-7892.

Medical Records

Accessing Your Medical Records

How to view, download, and request copies of your medical records.

Through Patient Portal:

  1. Log in to your patient portal account
  2. Navigate to "Medical Records" section
  3. View or download available records

Requesting Complete Records:

For complete medical records or records not available in the portal:

  1. Download and complete the Medical Records Request Form
  2. Submit the completed form to our Medical Records Department
  3. Allow 5-7 business days for processing

Note: There may be a fee for printed copies of medical records.

Understanding Your Test Results

A guide to interpreting common laboratory and diagnostic test results.

Your test results typically include:

  • Test Name: What was measured
  • Your Result: The measured value
  • Reference Range: Normal range for the test
  • Flag: Indicates if result is normal (N), high (H), or low (L)

Always discuss your test results with your healthcare provider for proper interpretation and next steps.

Preparing for Your Visit

What to Bring to Your Appointment

A checklist of items to bring to ensure a smooth visit.

Essential Items:

  • Photo ID (driver's license, passport, etc.)
  • Insurance card
  • List of current medications (including dosages)
  • List of allergies
  • Medical history information
  • Referral form (if required by your insurance)
  • Payment method for copays or deductibles

Additional Items:

  • Previous test results related to your visit
  • List of questions for your doctor
  • A family member or friend (if you'd like someone with you)

Preparing for Common Procedures

Guidelines for preparing for common tests and procedures.

Blood Tests:

  • Fast for 8-12 hours before the test (if instructed)
  • Drink plenty of water (unless instructed otherwise)
  • Continue taking medications (unless instructed otherwise)

Imaging Tests (X-ray, CT, MRI):

  • Wear comfortable clothing without metal
  • Remove jewelry and metal objects
  • Follow any specific preparation instructions

Note: Always follow the specific instructions provided by your healthcare provider, as preparation may vary based on your individual circumstances.
